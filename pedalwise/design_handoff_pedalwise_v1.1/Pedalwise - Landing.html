Three view modes

The same simulation, drawn three ways.

The kinematics never change. The rendering layer does. Start in Anatomical for the day-to-day. Switch to Diagnostic when something feels off; pull up Realistic when you want to present.

Anatomical

default · rider
Articulated segments. Joint dots. Angle labels at hip, knee, ankle. Reach envelope as a faint dashed circle. Nothing decorative — every mark is in service of a fit decision.

Diagnostic

fitters & coaches
Pedal force decomposition (tangential vs radial). Polar effectiveness plot. Joint power stack (hip / knee / ankle %). Detected-issue list ranked by severity.

Realistic

presentation
Filled silhouette with helmet, motion-blur trail, large HUD. Same kinematic solution under the hood — but lit for a presentation screen, not a calibration screen.
The model

One equation makes the trade-off real.

Speed, cadence and gear ratio are bound by a single closed-form relationship. Pin any two, the third is determined. The triangle widget lets you watch it work.

Drivetrain identity
v = (n/60) · G · π · D
vroad speed (m/s)
ncadence (rpm)
Ggear ratio (front / rear)
Dwheel diameter (m)
The metabolic-cost model layers on top — sensitive to cadence, knee flexion at BDC, and the fast-twitch fraction. Inverse-kinematics + Hill-type approximation, not a full musculoskeletal solver.

01Kinematics, then biomechanics

Inverse-kinematics solves the leg pose at every crank angle. Joint angles feed a closed-form efficiency model — gross η, IE, joint power share — in <16 ms per frame.

02Optimization is a dialogue

The optimizer runs in a Web Worker so the UI keeps animating. Results render as a ghost overlay — current at 25% opacity, optimum at 100%. You apply it, or you don't.

03Reduced-motion first-class

If your OS asks for reduced motion, the pedal stroke freezes until you press play. Numbers still update — they're the actual instrument; the animation is the metaphor.
