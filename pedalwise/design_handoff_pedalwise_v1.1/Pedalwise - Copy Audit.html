00 · Executive summary

The pattern: the model is correct. The copy keeps softening it.

Pedalwise's voice is closer to correct than most products at this stage — short sentences, honest numbers, no emoji. The remaining drift is in places where engineering language leaked into UI ("snapped from 173.1", "Best of 8 starts"), where the wrong-cycling word slipped in ("Endurance" used as a discipline, "gross effic." truncated), or where a hedge dulled an otherwise strong sentence ("Slightly outside Holmes range").

Five patterns to fix together

  1. Use the right cycling words in the right slots. "Endurance" is a training intensity, not a discipline. "Gross effic." is a truncation; "gross η" is the standard symbol. Match lib/presets.ts: Road · TT/Tri · XC MTB · Gravity MTB · Commuter.
  2. Hedges are out; numbers and directives are in. "Slightly outside Holmes range" → "above Holmes range". Pair every flagged value with the corrective action ("Try a higher saddle").
  3. Engineering breadcrumbs belong in tooltips, not in primary chrome. "snapped from 173.1", "Best of 8 starts", "(Sheldon)" — useful, but secondary. Demote to mono-italic notes or hide behind a hover.
  4. Headlines lead with the delta. The optimizer card already does this in the redesign (+3.2% in display type). Pull the same pattern into the in-app RecommendationPanel.
  5. Marketing copy is task-led, not audience-led. Landing page already removed "For engineers / For fitters / For DIY". Audit confirms the rewrite holds — but the nav anchor "Who it's for" still leaks the old framing. Renamed in the changeset.

CP-041 High

Knee-at-BDC issue · the hedge

components/DiagnosticPanels.tsx · issues
Current
Knee at BDC: 47°
Below Holmes range (target 25–45°) — consider lowering saddle.

"Slightly" in the wireframe — banned hedge. "Below" applies to angles < 25°, not 47°.

Revised
Knee at BDC: 47°
Above Holmes range (25–45°). Raise saddle 0.5–1.5 cm and re-measure.
Why 47° is above the range, not below. (Reading the code: there are two branches — <25° says "below" and >45° says "above" — but the wireframe screenshot uses "Slightly outside", which is the soft form. Production should drop "Slightly" and give a concrete adjustment range.) An "above-range knee at BDC" means too much extension; the corrective is a small saddle drop, not a raise. Fix the direction in the message too.
CP-042 Polish

Cadence-at-optimum issue · grammar

components/DiagnosticPanels.tsx
Current
Cadence in optimum
88 rpm, ideal for this power.
Revised
Cadence at optimum
88 rpm matches your power target.
Why "in optimum" is non-idiomatic. "at optimum" is the standard locution ("at peak", "at the optimum"). Bonus: "matches your power target" is more concrete than "ideal for this power".
CP-043 Polish

IE asymmetry message · casing

components/DiagnosticPanels.tsx
Current
IE asymmetry
Left 0.71, right 0.65 — minor

Mixed casing on "Left" / "right". Comma + em-dash combo.

Revised
IE asymmetry · minor
L 0.71 · R 0.65 · gap 0.06
Why Cycling convention is `L / R` for sides (power-meter readouts, shoe charts). Putting the severity adjective on the title line frees the body to carry the numbers cleanly. Adding the gap (`0.06`) makes the magnitude explicit for the engineer reader.
